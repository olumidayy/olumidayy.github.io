'Dear Favorite Author,'- Tribute to Karen Kingsbury

Karen Kingsbury Images Photo Credit: Google.

Whenever I pick up a Karen Kingsbury novel, two things are certain – Finishing it in a day and lots of tissue for tears.

Time Magazine called Mrs. Kingsbury the queen of Christian fiction. That title does seem to be well earned. She is a #1 New York Times bestselling novelist who has written or coauthored close to 100 novels and short stories. There are more than twenty-five million copies of her books in print. Many of her stories have been, or are in the process of being, made into movies. There is also a TV series under development with Roma Downey and Light Workers Media based on her popular Baxter Family books. In addition to all of that, Liberty University has established the Karen Kingsbury Center for Writing at which she serves as an adjunct professor. She also hosts her own radio program called appropriately enough, The Karen Kingsbury Show. As a public speaker she connects with more than 100,000 women at various events each year.

So how did she get started on her journey to become one of the most well-loved Christian authors of our day? If your picturing a little girl with a Bible in her hand and Jesus in her heart you would be wrong. She did have a love of books at an early age. As a child her family moved a lot. While she would have to leave friends behind she would forever be connected to the stories she read. She had a particular fondness for the works of Dr. Seuss. Mrs. Kingsbury received a journalism degree from Cal State University Northridge. Following this she worked at the Los Angeles Times as a sports reporter. Later she would also write about crime.

In 1991 Mrs. Kingsbury had her first book published. Missy’s Murder was a true crime story based on a murder that she covered as a reporter. She authored three other true crime books before trying her hand at a novel. She had trouble finding a publisher for Where Yesterday Lives. Mrs. Kingsbury received thirty rejection letters before Multnomah Publishers decided to give her a try. In an interview with CBN she explains why Christian fiction has become so popular over the last few years. “Secular writers can tell a story about the physical, the emotional, and the intellectual parts of a character. But no matter how well they tell the story, they miss a facet that is innately part of all of us – the spiritual. As a Christian fiction writer, I can tell a story about the complete person, the whole character. I think this resonates with readers, and touches them in the deepest parts of their hearts and souls.” In the same interview she told about her process for writing. “I write one novel at a time, but I can research multiple titles at once. I take about six to eight weeks to write a novel, once the research is finished.”

Personally, I have read 90% of all her books and I know for a surety that Karen Kingsbury is the Author I hope to be. She inspires me not only to write but to understand the gift of family, love and friendship which has helped me in my relationships today. The Baxter Family and Bailey Flanigan series are my favorite. Karen Kingsbury has had a remarkable life and an extraordinary career. She has entertained and encouraged countless readers, primarily women. Along the way she has inspired a few of us men as well. Thank you, Karen for the many volumes of life-changing fiction. Long live the queen!
